Our Mission

A four-day case competition drawing together students from universities around the world, SLASummit sets out to not only highlight major issues facing Latin American communities but cultivate ideas proposing tangible solutions to these problems. By bringing together academic experts in related fields and social entrepreneurs with real-world experience, to give both TED-style talks and interactive workshops, the conference emerges as a unique platform to develop innovative and creative projects that will be sustainably implemented and maintained for years to come. Differentiating itself as more than just a case competition to apply critical thinking and presentation skills, the purpose of SLASummit is to the develop winning ideas into solutions that will visibly improve the lives of Latin American communities in need.

Our Story

Our Roots

SLASummit emerged as a TED-style conference focused on raising awareness about current Latin American issues and establishing networks between McGill and the Montreal Latin American community. Transitioning from a descriptive dialogue to a prescriptive one, SLASummit began looking for ways of making a greater impact.

Our inspiration

Concurrently, SLASA, the Spanish and Latin American Students' Association of McGill University, embarked on a social project in Mexico to help a community of primary school children get to school faster. With a budget of $5,000, we not only provided bicycles for 30 children, reducing their transport time by more than half, but we also implemented a bike rental business with its own repair garage to ensure sustainability.


With this past example of bottom-up, community based success, SLASA decided to revamp the conference as a networking and launch platform for development projects. By bringing together passionate students and leading professionals, SLASummit develops targeted solutions, promoting cooperation and dialogue, to specific Latin American issues.


Our Approach

Sustainable Development

SLASummit holds a firm belief in bottom-up, self-empowering forms of development. The proposed solutions will not only be the answers to problems communicated directly by the community, but also selected and implemented in conjunction with the local population. By taking this participatory approach, we not only ensure long-term sustainability but also improve the efficiency of implemented projects, catering to communities’ needs as specifically as possible. Moreover, we reach more complete answers by strengthening the development of ideas as they must be deemed viable from the different perspectives of students and experts from a diverse group of participants.

Our Objectives

    The objectives of the SLASummit are to

  • Raise awareness about current political, social and economic issues in Latin America
  • Build multidisciplinary problem-solving skills necessary to address societal challenges
  • Attract and connect the brightest participants and professionals to devise & implement viable solutions to foster sustainable development in Latin America
  • Turn Latin America and Montreal into a hub of innovation and entrepreneurship of the world

Gil Peñalosa

 Gil Peñalosa is passionate about cities for all people. He advises decision makers and communities on how to create vibrant cities and healthy communities for everyone. Because of his unique blend of pragmatism and passion, Gil’s leadership and advice is sought out by many cities and organizations, giving him the opportunity to work in more than 180 different cities across six continents. He is also an accomplished presenter and inspirational speaker. Mr. Peñalosa is the Founder and Chair of the Board of the successful non-profit organization 8-80 Cities. He also works as Urban Expert for the Danish firm Gehl Architects and serves on the Board of Directors of City Parks Alliance. Gil holds an MBA from UCLA’s Anderson School of Management, where he recently was selected as one of the “100 Most Inspirational Alumni” in the school’s history. In 2014 he received a Doctorate Honoris Causa from the Faculty of Landscape Architecture and Urban Planning at the prominent University of Sweden SLU.
